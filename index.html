The Problem

Security built for a world
that no longer exists.

Perimeters are gone. Data moves constantly — across employees, contractors, partners, and AI systems. Existing tools were not built for any of it.

Control breaks at the boundary

Once a file leaves your network — to a partner, a personal device, or a collaborator's cloud — enforcement becomes theoretical. Revocation rarely works. Visibility disappears.

AI is moving data faster than governance can keep up

Blanket blocking drives shadow AI. Doing nothing bleeds data. 57% of employees already input sensitive information into public AI tools — with personal accounts, outside any policy.

Security vs. business velocity — a losing tradeoff

The tighter the controls, the slower the work. Teams route around security to get things done. The result: a deteriorating relationship between security and the business it's supposed to protect.

"Traditional DLP watches the door.
We make the data useless if it walks out."
Our Approach

Protection that travels
with the data.

Tika is the data control layer that lets enterprise data move freely — across people, devices, and AI — without sacrificing security or governance.

01

Control follows the data, not the perimeter

Protection stays enforceable even when a file leaves your network, your approved apps, or your organization entirely. The boundary is no longer the last line of defense.

02

Centralized policy, decentralized enforcement

Define rules once. Enforce them everywhere — in the flow of work, without asking people to change how they operate.

03

AI governed like humans — not blocked or ignored

AI agents are treated as first-class actors under the same access control model as people. Granular, auditable, and policy-governed — not a binary allow/block that breaks workflows.

04

Meaningful revocation

Access can be stopped after the fact. Once revoked, the next access is denied — even for data already across a boundary, on a device you don't own.
