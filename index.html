The Problem

Security built for a world
that no longer exists.

The perimeter is gone. Data moves constantly — across employees, contractors, partners, and AI systems. The tools enterprises rely on weren't built for any of it.

01

Control breaks at every boundary

Once a file leaves your network — to a partner, a personal device, or an AI tool — enforcement becomes theoretical. Revocation rarely works. Visibility disappears entirely.

02

AI is moving data faster than governance

Blanket blocking drives shadow AI. Doing nothing bleeds data. 57% of employees already input sensitive information into public AI tools, outside any policy or oversight.

03

Security and velocity in constant conflict

The tighter the controls, the slower the work. Teams route around security to get things done. The result is a deteriorating relationship between security and the business it exists to protect.

What We Believe

Security shouldn't be a tax
on getting work done.

The best security is invisible to people who follow the rules, and impossible to bypass for everyone else.

Value in the right hands

Data delivers outcomes when it reaches the people and AI systems that can act on it. Locking everything down isn't security — it's friction that costs the business.

Frictionless by default

Sharing shouldn't require heroics, training, or perfect behavior. Security that depends on employees doing the right thing every time will always fail.

Security travels with the work

Protection holds as data moves across teams, companies, devices, and tools. The boundary is not the last line of defense — the data itself is.

Trust you can prove

Clear boundaries and auditable decisions, so control is credible — not assumed. Compliance is built into how data moves, not generated after the fact.

Our Approach

A control layer that travels
with the data.

Tika enforces access policy at the data layer — not at the network edge. Control holds wherever data goes, on any device, in any app, for any actor including AI.

01

Control follows the data, not the perimeter

Policy stays enforceable even when a file leaves your network, your approved apps, or your organization entirely. The boundary is no longer the last line of defense.

02

Centralized policy, decentralized enforcement

Define access rules once. Enforce them everywhere — in the flow of work, without asking people to change how they operate or install new tools.

03

AI governed like humans — not blocked or ignored

AI agents are treated as first-class actors under the same access control model as people. Granular, auditable, policy-governed — not a binary allow/block.

04

Revocation that actually works

Access can be stopped after the fact. Once revoked, the next access is denied — even for data already across a boundary, on a device you don't own.
